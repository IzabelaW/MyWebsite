Semester VI

Mathematical analysis I

What I've learned?

  1. I finally understood the relation between integral and derivative.
  2. I know how to use L'Hôpital's rule to check if f=O(g).
  3. I learned what a power series is.

What should I train more?

I feel that the theory of functions of a complex variable is worth learning. I would also like to learn the Lebesgue integral.

Logic and formal structures

What I've learned?

  1. I can apply the principle of Mathematical Induction.
  2. I know the concept of a well-order.
  3. I understand the idea od finitary relation, function and I learned what the equivalence relation is.

What should I train more?

I'd like to learn more about Peano axioms and modal logic.

Algebra with analytic geometry

What I've learned?

  1. I learned about groups, fields and rings. I know the concept of polynomial and the fundamental theorem of algebra.
  2. I can solve systems of linear equations, calculate invertible matrix, determine coordinates of Euklidean vectors in basis and dimension of linear subspace.
  3. I can finally do operations with complex numbers.

What should I train more?

I suppose that Gram–Schmidt process is something I should dedicate some time to.

Introduction to computer science and programming

What I've learned?

  1. I got to know divide-and-conquer as a technique of constructing effective algorithms.
  2. I finally acquired the skill of practical programming in C language.
  3. I learned about pros and cons of the recursion.

What should I train more?

It was just an introduction so I hope don't have any shortcomings.

MY HOBBY

TRAVELLING

To travel is to live!


Traveling is one of my favorite hobbies. I love visiting new countries (especially those exoitic ones like Croatia), seeing historical sites, and experiencing new cultures. Throughout my life I have been blessed to travel to a variety of different places.

During my travels, I love to learn about history and art. I love museums and “tourist” traps. Every time you travel a whole new world is opened and a whole new way of thinking. In order to be a productive citizen of the world, you need to learn about what there is in the world and appreciate the life other people live. I can’t believe how diverse the world is and what it has to offer.

One of my personal hobbies is to visit European capitals. I've already been in Prague, Vienna, Budapest, Bratislava and (my latest trip!) Paris. These are usually weekend (short but very intense) trips which I organize by myself. I've never bought a trip via travel aggency. If you do so, you probably miss the biggest fun!
